Source strategy

Global Trend Sources Guide

Ming Trends is designed around overseas discovery behavior. Instead of copying one platform's hot list, the site groups sources by user intent: search demand, social debate, creator attention, developer adoption, shopping discovery, entertainment, and gaming.

Search intent sources

Google Trends is the strongest source for SEO-led pages because it reveals what people are actively searching for. Use it to create explainers, FAQ pages, comparison posts, and evergreen updates. Pair it with Google Search Console after launch.

Social discussion sources

Reddit and X are useful for early controversy, breaking topics, sports rumors, fandom activity, finance debates, and public sentiment. Treat them as discovery sources, not final verification. Link to primary sources when possible.

Video and creator sources

YouTube, TikTok Creative Center, Instagram, and Twitch show what audiences are watching, remixing, and sharing. These sources work well for entertainment, product demos, gaming, music, sports highlights, and creator economy topics.

Developer and startup sources

Hacker News, GitHub Trending, and Product Hunt help identify early technical adoption. They are especially valuable for AI tools, open-source projects, SaaS launches, security incidents, infrastructure, and startup products.

Entertainment and commerce sources

IMDb, Rotten Tomatoes, Spotify Charts, Steam Charts, Amazon Best Sellers, and app store rankings help build topic pages around media demand, consumer behavior, and seasonal purchasing intent.

Recommended refresh cadence

  • Breaking social trends: every 15 to 60 minutes.
  • Search and SEO trends: every 4 to 24 hours.
  • Product, GitHub, and startup trends: daily.
  • Entertainment, gaming, and shopping charts: daily or weekly depending on source volatility.

Compliance notes

For AdSense and long-term SEO, do not publish scraped lists without added value. Each trend page should include summary, source attribution, context, update time, and original editorial analysis.
