Source transparency

News Sources Guide

Ming Journal uses public, documented feeds to discover current reporting. We show a headline, a source name, a timestamp, and a direct path back to the original publisher. We do not republish a publisher's article body as a feed item.

GDELT Project

GDELT is a public global database of news coverage and events. The live desk queries its article list API for recent English-language reporting. GDELT helps with discovery; the linked publisher remains the primary reporting source.

Hacker News public API

Hacker News is used as a graceful technical-news fallback when the main public feed is unavailable. It is useful for developer, startup, security, science, and open-web discussion, but discussion velocity is not verification.

How we use feeds

Feeds are a starting point. They help editors identify reporting that may deserve a reading note, a source check, or a longer original explainer. A feed item is never treated as a complete account of a developing event.

Source attribution

Every live card identifies the upstream service that surfaced it and the publisher hosting the original link. When an item is expanded, readers receive a direct link to that publisher and a reminder to check its reporting, updates, and corrections.

Refresh cadence

  • Live news APIs are requested on page load with a short timeout.
  • Failed primary requests fall back to a secondary public endpoint.
  • Production deployments should cache normalized responses server-side.
  • Original editorial pages should state their update time and source links.

Compliance notes

For AdSense and long-term SEO, do not publish copied or scraped articles. Each original Ming Journal page should add clear context, source attribution, update time, and independently written editorial value.
